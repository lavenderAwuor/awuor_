ABOUT US

MAA Widows, Orphans, and Vulnerable Children is a community-based organization (CBO) with the Registration Number NRK/HDM/03640. It was formally registered in 2022. Currently, our organization comprises 300 widows distributed across 10 wards within the county, with approximately 30 widows in each ward.

VISION

"A World Where Widows Are Embraced By Justice,Their Rights Safeguarded,and Their Dignity Respected"

MISSION

To provide comprehensive support and resources to widows, empowering them to rebuild their lives with dignity, resilience, and independence. Through a combination of psychosocial assistance, economic empowerement programs,legal advocacy, and community awareness campaigns

CORE VALUES

Dignity and Respect

Inclusivity

Transparency

Client-Centered Approach

Integrity

Accountability

FOCUS AREAS

    Offer Psychosocial Support to the Widows.

    Promote Economic Empowerment Among the Widows.

    Sensitize WIdows on Succession and Inheritance Laws.

    Raising HIV/AIDS awareness among widows.

    Support Children and Orphans Through Provision of Basic Needs and Paying School Fees

    Raise Awareness on Female Genital Mutilation(FGM) in the Community.

    Raise Awareness on clean water use

    Environment Conservation
